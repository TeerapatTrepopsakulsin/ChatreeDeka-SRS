\section{Stakeholder Analysis}
\label{section:stakeholder-analysis}

\subsection{Direct Users / End-Users}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Law Students:} Need a realistic, interactive environment to study and practice civil procedure. They are directly affected by the UI complexity and accuracy of the courtroom flow.
    \item \textbf{Lawyers and Legal Practitioners:} Require an accurate tool to evaluate civil case winning potential based on facts. Their adoption depends on the simulator's alignment with the Civil and Commercial Code.
    \item \textbf{Judiciary Trainees and Court Staff:} Need to practice managing civil trials and drafting judgments. They require high-fidelity simulations of actual courtroom environments.
    \item \textbf{Litigants:} Want to simulate real-world disputes to understand their legal standing before hiring a lawyer. They represent a non-technical user base that relies on simplified legal terminology.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{External Influencers}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Lawyers Council of Thailand:} Concerned with maintaining ethical standards and preventing the unauthorized practice of law. Their public stance could heavily influence professional adoption.
    \item \textbf{Office of the Judiciary / Court Administration:} Govern court procedures and are interested in modernizing legal training. The software's workflows must strictly align with their official administrative and procedural rules.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Beneficiaries or Indirect Users}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Law Firm Clients:} Desire successful and cost-effective resolution of their disputes. Their financial future can be influenced by the lawyer's use of the simulator.
    \item \textbf{The Justice System:} Aims to reduce court backlogs. Benefited indirectly if the tool helps users realize a case is weak, thereby discouraging frivolous lawsuits from being filed.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{User Stories}

\subsubsection{Law Students}
\begin{itemize}
    \item As a law student, I want to manually control the Prosecutor Agent in a simulated trial so that I can practice my case-building, argumentation, and cross-examination dynamically.
    \item As a law student, I want to input evidence and conversation dynamically during a live mock trial so that I can learn how new information shifts the trial's direction in real-time.
    \item As a law student, I want to raise procedural objections against the MARL-trained Defender during the trial so that I can test my knowledge of Thai civil procedure rules.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Lawyers and Legal Practitioners}
\begin{itemize}
    \item As a legal practitioner, I want to run a hybrid simulation against a MARL-trained Defender Agent so that I can stress-test my case arguments against realistic, auto-generated counter-arguments.
    \item As a legal practitioner, I want the Case Predictor Agent to analyze the completed trial simulation and output a win probability percentage so that I can evaluate case viability before accepting a client.
    \item As a legal practitioner, I want the system to automatically suggest relevant Supreme Court precedents during the post-trial feedback phase so that I can strengthen my real-world legal strategy.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Judiciary Trainees and Court Staff}
\begin{itemize}
    \item As a judiciary trainee, I want to act as the manual Judge Agent while the AI handles the Prosecutor and Defender roles so that I can practice ruling on procedural objections and managing trial flow.
    \item As a judiciary trainee, I want to review the post-trial analysis generated by the Case Predictor so that I can compare my manual trial rulings against the system's objective legal evaluation.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Litigants / General Public}
\begin{itemize}
    \item As a litigant, I want plain-language guidance prompts during simulation setup so that I can input my Thai Civil Law dispute details without deciphering complex legal jargon.
    \item As a litigant, I want the Case Predictor Agent to provide a simplified summary of potential legal outcomes post-simulation so that I can decide if it is worth hiring a lawyer.
    \item As a litigant, I want the system to suggest alternative dispute resolution (ADR) options based on the simulation results so that I can explore cheaper ways to settle my conflict outside of court.
\end{itemize}
